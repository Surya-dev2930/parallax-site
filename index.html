Aliyar Dam

About Aliyar Dam

Aliyar Dam is a Charming Location surrounded by annamali hills with wonderful natural views. It is located between Pollachi and Valparai. At a distance of 64 Kilometers from Coimbatore, 24 kms from Pollachi & 545 kms from chennai.

Built across Aliyar river between 1959 and 1969, the primary purpose of dam is to support irrigation for pollachi and near by places for Agriculture purpose. The height of the Dam is 81 meters. There is well maintained park built at the bottom of the Dam. The view of the anamalai range around the resevoir is a feast for the eyes and the view of cocnut groves from top of the dam is amazing. Boating is available here.

About the city

About District

The third largest city of the state, Coimbatore,is one of the most industrialized cities in Tamil Nadu, known as the textile capital of South India or the Manchester of the South, the city is situated on the banks of the river Noyyal, Coimbatore existed even prior to the 2nd or 3rd century AD by Karikalan, the first of the early Cholas. Among its other great rulers were Rashtrakutas, Chalukyas, Pandyas, Hoysalas and the Vijayanagara kings. When Kongunadu fell to the British along with the rest of the state, its name was changed to Coimbatore and it is by this name that it is known today, except in Tamil, in which it is called Kovai.
